Herbivores or plant life

Herbivores, or plant-eating animals, are living organisms that rely primarily or entirely on plant matter for their food, such as grasses, leaves, stems, fruits, seeds, and sometimes algae. These organisms do not feed on meat as their primary source of energy, but rather obtain their nutritional needs from organic compounds produced by plants through photosynthesis. Herbivores possess physical and functional characteristics that enable them to efficiently consume and digest plants. They typically have broad, flat teeth designed to grind plant fibers rather than tear flesh, and their digestive system is longer than that of carnivores because plant matter—especially fiber-rich materials like cellulose—requires more time to decompose and digest. Some herbivores, such as cows and sheep, have a multi-chambered stomach that allows specialized bacteria to ferment their food before it is fully digested.
